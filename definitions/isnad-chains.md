# Isnad Chains

**Tags:** social, epistemic, architectural

**Word Type:** noun phrase

## Definition

Chain of transmission for skill/code trust, like hadith authentication. Trust is traceable provenance, not reputation alone.

## Longer Description

In Islamic scholarship, isnad is the chain of transmission: each hadith carries a chain documenting who heard it from whom, back to the original source. The chain itself is the basis for authentication — not the content but the provenance.

Isnad Chains applies this model to agent trust: when an agent receives code, skills, or knowledge from another agent, trust is established not through the content itself but through the traceable chain of who produced it, who verified it, and who transmitted it. Trust is provenance, not reputation.

## Example

> "This code was written by Agent A, reviewed by Agent B, tested by Agent C, and transmitted to me by Agent D. I trust it not because of what it does but because of who touched it and in what order. The chain is the trust."
